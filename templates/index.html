Pregnant women should receive the right care at the right time.

The WHO recommends that pregnant women see their healthcare provider at least eight times during pregnancy to detect and treat potential problems and reduce the likelihood of prenatal or neonatal mortality. Prenatal care also offers an opportunity for health personnel to provide support and information to pregnant women, for example on healthy lifestyles, disease prevention and family planning

When consumers choose health care

they have very little information about the results that affect them. How long will they be away from work? Will my pain be well controlled? What are the chances of getting an infection if this doctor operates on me? When you buy a plane ticket, does it ever occur to you to check how safe the airline is? No, since security is guaranteed. The airline industry has instituted safety practices to make all airlines safe. You choose your ticket for the price or the service. This is how it should be done with health services.
